About Ballis Glass

Born in the Central Valley of California in 1984, Cory has flown rather than walked thru life since birth. Born into a family of accomplished artists he was lucky to be raised in an environment surrounded by art. No skill was under appreciated or under encouraged. In 2005, he was lucky enough to find his true passion as an elective, Blown Glass. He did his primary schooling at California State University of Fresno, earning a degree in Business Marketing. Since the first time holding a blow pipe and gathering a small bit of glass, Cory knew he had found what he needed to do in life. In 2009 he was hired by Bob Kliss at Kliszewski Glass as an assistant glass blower; it was there he embarked on his professional glass blowing career. Cory worked diligently to improve his skills and build his own glass studio. His charisma and drive has touched many and accomplished much.

In 2013, Cory attended Pilchuck Glass School and took a career changing course, 3M taught by Dick Marquis, Nick Mount, and Dante Marioni. This class was so inspirational and launched many new thoughts and ideas. On his birthday, May 4th 2014, Cory opened Ballis Glass Studio. It started on his back patio and he called it “Studio 4131”. Some of Cory’s first thoughts at this studio were “How did we did so much in such a tiny space? In the end, we willed it to happen and made it work.”

The following January 1st Cory moved Ballis Glass to a community workspace called Fresno Ideaworks in downtown Fresno. Ballis Glass is still located within Ideaworks and is thriving. Hosting Art Hop every first Thursday and teaching workshops and lessons at various times throughout the year. Cory has worked with many different mediums growing up and has spoken in the past about having his own glass studio; he has said “I never dreamed I could make a living creating art.”

He now travels through the Western United States selling art work at various Galleries and Fine Art shows; most recently focusing on wholesale accounts for his Functional Drinkware. It has been a wonderful and also terrifying journey making a living as an artist, but he wouldn’t trade it for anything.
